Things You Should Know
When Planting A Tree Or Shrub

1. Call Before You Dig - Several days before
planting call Dig Alert at 1-800-422-4133.
This is especially necessary when planting
large material.

2. Treat With Care - It is always important to
handle the plant or tree by the root ball or
container. This will ensure that the plant is
not damaged.

3. Dig A Proper Hole - Make sure to dig the
hole 2-3 times the width of the root ball or
container. This will ensure that the plant will
have proper conditions for its root system to
establish.

4. Proper Planting Depth - The crown of the
trunk or plant should always be an inch or
two above the finished grade level. It is very
important to make sure the plant is not
planted too low. It is better to plant it a little
too high than too low

5. Backfilling The Hole - It is very important
to backfill the hole with native soil mixed
with proper soil amendments appropriate
for the plant (e.g. mulch for a tree and peat
moss for an Azalea). Also, make sure to
tamp the soil gently to eliminate air pockets
and reduce settling.

6. Top Dressing - It is always recommended
to top dress around the new planting with an
inch of mulch. This will give the plant some
additional nutrition as well as condition
the soil around it.
